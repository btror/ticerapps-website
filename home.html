About the Developer

Brandon is a Junior attending the University of North Carolina at Charlotte. He is working on his bachelors degree in Computer Science with a software engineering concentration.

During the summer before his Sophomore year at the Unviersity of North Carolina at Charlotte had started, Brandon had decided to learn more ways to put the Java skills that he had developed in the first two introduction to computer science courses to practice. He started off by creating GUI applications using Java Swing components. He made everything from games to utilities he thought people would find useful. After creating several GUI's, he thought that it would be a good idea to figure out how to make some kind of a product from the projects he worked on.

After doing a little bit of research he concluded that it would be best to create mobile application versions of the GUI's he made. This was good for several reasons, one being that it was an opportunity to learn new technologies. Another reason was because the coding process is very similar to using Java Swing. As a matter of fact, the apps were coded using Java. The difference was that Swing was not being used. Instead, he used an Android SDK and the Android Studio JDE, rather than Netbeans. He also had to learn how to use some markup languages, Gradle, and photoshop to design icons and other important images. The biggest reason why he chose to make mobile applications was because he was interested to see if people would actually download and use his apps, which hundreds did.
